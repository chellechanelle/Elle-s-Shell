When a Poet Falls In Love

When a poet falls in love,
The world turns around,
Falls on its knees
For it to be spared
Of the anguish that
Will soon be planted
Upon it.

When a poet falls in love,
the earth becomes their journal,
Pressing their feet into the
Mushy sand with the
Grooves of their shoes,
They are silent,
Yet loud
With their mutterings of words
And all the things left unsaid.
The tree became
Their paper,
Whose brittle rough bark
The poet sways their
Hand against
The world like braille,
In preparation to release
The words that feel as if they are
Pouring out of their hearts,
Like a dam once its walls fall,
Like an infinite waterfall.

Because when someone grabs
The key to their heart,
Wanted or not,
It streams into
A flood of tears
With no boundaries set,
Of words left unsaid,
Of their lifetime,
And those of their ancestors,
And uncountable
Moments before.

The words can’t be contained,
as they just stream out
In the form of slurred words–
There is no path
That they wouldn’t cover.
It flows to their heart,
Then to their eyes,
Then to their nerves,
Controlling the scribbles
Of their hands
And encapsulating every inch of their body
Telling them that the
Only reasonable thing they
Could do in this moment
Is shut the door
Wrap their hands around
The rough calluses
Of a pencil lying around
Grab the silky paper
Lying on the floor,
And start an infinite loop
As the words flow
through their body and their pencil
Onto an empty canvas,
Eager for a story to be written
And a truth to be told.

Because when a poet falls in love,
You become their world,
And their only clear explanation
Is to harbor this
Love in their heart
For so long
Until they realize
This love is not a ship
On a dock
That can be harbored
Or a lightswitch
That can turn this
Beautiful, strikingly deadly light
On and off as they choose.

No, this love is
So strong, yet it makes them weak
So impalpable, yet seen
And felt,
Like a sudden cold chill
On a summer day,
And so paralyzing,
Yet their blood pulses
Through their veins
A million times per second
The moment even
The mere thought of their love
Appears in their mind,
That their only option
Is to let these rivers
Of love flow
From a heartfelt prayer
Swaying the earth
And all the things
They could never say
To the one person
That makes
their world.
