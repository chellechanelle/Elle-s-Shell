Older

My mother doesn’t check
My room at night anymore,
I hear the door knob twist
And a creak
As my mother opens
My younger sisters room
Next door.
I hear the slight ruffle
Of the sheets as she
Adjusts my sisters covering
To make sure her nose is not covered.
I hear the tap of her fingers
On the nightlight
To make sure the light
Is not too bright for her to sleep.
I hear the pacing around the room,
Perhaps she is looking to make sure
My sisters outfit is set
Maybe pink tights and a white cartoon tee,
so she is ready
For the next day at school.

There is a sharp breath.
The door creaks open again
Before it slams shut.
I pull my blankets
Over my nose
And turn to my side,
Burying myself deeper.

I hear footsteps in the hall.
Any second now
My door would creak
Open,
And she will check
If all my clothes are packed,
If my nightlight,
(which I’m far too old to be using) Is too bright.
I already have the questions to ask
In my head:
A thousand random
Questions that popped up in
My head during my shower.

But the creak never came.

I hear the footsteps creep
Further in the hall.
I hear another creak
A distance away.
The door slams shut.

Is it because I’m getting older?
Is it because of the countless days
I begged for my own room,
Own space,
Own privacy?
Is that why?
Isn't that what I wanted?
To be older?

But in this moment
I don’t want to be older
anymore.
I want to be a little girl
floating among the lavender
wandering aimlessly around the fields
knowing someone is out there
always worried for me
and that I’m always being
watched over,
protected.
I don’t want to be older
anymore,
because it feels like
the older I get,
the more harsh winds
life decides to hurl at me,
the more isolated
I become
from my family
my friends
and the little girl
inside of me
whom I once loved.
